GSP Eniel — Real Estate Website (UX/UI & WordPress Development)

Project Summary:
Designed and developed a real estate website focused on clarity, usability, and conversion. The project prioritized intuitive navigation, scannable property listings, and a mobile-first experience, built on WordPress using the Houzez theme.

Role:
UX/UI Designer & WordPress Developer (End-to-End)

Client:
GSP Eniel — Real Estate Services

Project Type:
Real Estate Website

Problem:
Users needed a clear and efficient way to browse property listings, quickly understand key information, and take action without friction. The client also lacked a professional digital presence to establish trust with potential buyers and renters.

Users & Goals:
Primary Users: Property buyers and renters
User Goals: Browse listings easily, compare properties, and contact an agent
Business Goals: Increase inquiries and build brand credibility

UX/UI Design Decisions:
– Designed property layouts for fast visual scanning
– Established clear visual hierarchy to highlight key property details
– Placed call-to-action buttons strategically to reduce decision friction
– Applied consistent spacing and typography to lower cognitive load
– Designed mobile-first layouts for users browsing on the go

Design & Development:
Customized the Houzez WordPress theme to support UX goals rather than relying on default layouts. Built responsive pages using Elementor, configured property listing functionality, and optimized the site for performance and SEO readiness.

Constraints & Trade-offs:
Working within a pre-built theme required balancing flexibility with development efficiency. Visual customizations were intentionally limited in favor of usability, clarity, and loading speed.

Outcome & Impact:
– Fully responsive real estate website successfully launched
– Improved clarity and usability of property listings
– Professional digital presence aligned with both user and business needs

What I Learned:
Designing within constraints strengthened decision-making and reinforced the importance of user-centered thinking over visual complexity.
